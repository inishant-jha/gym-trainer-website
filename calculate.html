Understanding BMR & TDEE

What is BMR?

Basal Metabolic Rate (BMR) is the number of calories your body needs to accomplish its most basic (basal) life-sustaining functions at complete rest. This includes breathing, circulation, cell production, and nutrient processing.

What is TDEE?

Total Daily Energy Expenditure (TDEE) is an estimate of how many calories you burn per day when exercise is taken into account. It's calculated by multiplying your BMR by your activity level multiplier.

How It's Calculated

We use the Mifflin-St Jeor Equation, which is considered the most accurate BMR formula for adults:

Men: BMR = 10 × weight(kg) + 6.25 × height(cm) - 5 × age(y) + 5

Women: BMR = 10 × weight(kg) + 6.25 × height(cm) - 5 × age(y) - 161

Disclaimer: This calculator provides estimates only. Individual results may vary based on factors like genetics, muscle mass, and medical conditions. Always consult with a healthcare professional before making significant changes to your diet or exercise routine.
